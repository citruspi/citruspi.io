Hi there!

I'm Mihir, but I go by @citruspi on the Internet.

I'm a student at the Rochester Institute of Technology pursuing a degree in Computing Security. I'm also a member of the Computer Science House, a special interest house, and the FOSS Box, an applied research laboratory.

I spend a lot of my time working on personal projects, all of which are open source. I'm passionate about open source work and the public domain, which many of my projects are dedicated to. I previously interned at Apple, Inc. where I worked within the GPU Software Engineering Organization. Since January 2015, I've been interning at Hudl, where I work on infrastructure.

When I have the time, I also enjoy fiction and nonfiction reading material. I grew up on, and continue to love, the Tintin and Asterix and Obelix comics. Unfortunately, I don't get much reading time at this point.

I also spend a lot of my time travelling. Not as much as I'd like to though. I've visited twenty six U.S. states and ten countries (including the U.S.). I've also lived in Mumbai, India, Hanoi, Vietnam, Paris, France, Vancouver, Canada, Rochester, New York, Sunnyvale, California, Lincoln, Nebraska, and Boston, Massachusetts.

My primary tools for software development would include git, vim, a 13" MacBook Air, Python, Go, tmux, and servers running either CentOS or Amazon Linux. Every year, I think it'll finally be the year of Linux, and every year it isn't. Depending on the setting, I may be using Apple EarPods or ATH-M50x headphones.

My primary tools for photography would be my Nikon D5300 with the following lenses - AF-S DX NIKKOR 35mm f/1.8G, Tokina AT-X 11-16mm f/2.8 PRO DX-II, and AF-S VR Zoom-Nikkor 70-300mm f/4.5-5.6G IF-ED - and Adobe Lightroom running on a 15" MacBook Pro (w/Retina). I frequently use a Manfrotto Compaction Action tripod and I carry all of this around in a Manfrotto Veloce backpack.

On the Internet, you can find me on Twitter and GitHub. I used to use App.net, but well…. And I used to use last.fm, but Apple Music Radio doesn't suport it. I also hang out on freenode.
